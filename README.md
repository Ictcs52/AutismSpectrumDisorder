## üìã How to Use

### Step-by-Step Guide
1. **Start Assessment**: Open the application in your web browser
2. **Read Instructions**: Review the comprehensive instructions and age requirements
3. **Answer Questions**: Complete all 10 questions about your child's behavior
   - Each question has 4 possible answers: Always, Usually, Sometimes, Never
   - Questions cover various behavioral aspects related to autism screening
4. **Track Progress**: Monitor your progress with the real-time progress bar
5. **Calculate Results**: Click "Calculate Results" to see your total score
6. **Review Recommendations**: Read the detailed recommendations based on your score
7. **Retake if Needed**: Use the "Take Assessment Again" button to restart

### Tips for Accurate Assessment
- Answer based on your child's typical behavior over the past few months
- Consider your child's behavior in various settings (home, daycare, social situations)
- Take your time and reflect on each question carefully
- Don't rush through the assessment - accuracy is important

## üìä Scoring System

### Scoring Method
The Q-Chat-10 uses a specific scoring system based on research-validated criteria:

**Questions A1-A9 (9 questions):**
- **Sometimes**: 1 point
- **Rarely**: 1 point  
- **Never**: 1 point
- **Always/Usually**: 0 points

**Question A10 (1 question):**
- **Always**: 1 point
- **Usually**: 1 point
- **Sometimes**: 1 point
- **Never**: 0 points

### Score Interpretation
- **Total Possible Score**: 0-10 points
- **Low Risk (0-3 points)**: Typical development patterns
- **Elevated Risk (4+ points)**: Recommended for professional evaluation
- **Critical Threshold**: Score above 3 suggests need for professional consultation

### What Your Score Means
- **Score 0-3**: Your child's responses suggest typical developmental patterns. Continue monitoring development.
- **Score 4+**: Your child's responses indicate some behaviors that may warrant further evaluation by a healthcare professional.

### Important Notes
- This is a screening tool, not a diagnostic instrument
- Results should be discussed with a pediatrician or developmental specialist
- Early intervention, when needed, can significantly improve outcomes

## üî¨ About the Q-Chat-10

### Research Background
The Q-Chat-10 (Quantitative Checklist for Autism in Toddlers) is a validated screening instrument developed for early detection of autism spectrum disorder in children aged 18-24 months. This tool is based on extensive research and clinical validation studies.

### Key Research Points
- **Validation Studies**: Extensively tested across diverse populations
- **Age Range**: Specifically designed for 18-24 month old children
- **Sensitivity**: High accuracy in identifying children who may benefit from further evaluation
- **Clinical Use**: Widely adopted by healthcare professionals worldwide

### What the Q-Chat-10 Measures
The questionnaire evaluates key behavioral indicators:
- **Social Communication**: Eye contact, pointing, social interest
- **Repetitive Behaviors**: Unusual interests, repetitive actions
- **Sensory Processing**: Responses to sensory stimuli
- **Developmental Milestones**: Age-appropriate social and communication skills

## üè• Clinical Information

### When to Use This Tool
- **Primary Screening**: Initial assessment for autism spectrum concerns
- **Regular Check-ups**: Monitoring developmental milestones
- **Parental Concerns**: When parents notice unusual behaviors

### What This Tool Cannot Do
- **Diagnose Autism**: Only healthcare professionals can make diagnoses
- **Replace Professional Assessment**: This is a screening tool only
- **Provide Treatment Plans**: Clinical intervention requires professional guidance
- **Guarantee Accuracy**: Results should always be interpreted by professionals

### Next Steps After Assessment
If your score suggests further evaluation:
1. **Contact Your Pediatrician**: Discuss results with your child's doctor
2. **Seek Specialist Referral**: Request evaluation by developmental pediatrician
3. **Early Intervention**: Consider early childhood intervention services
4. **Support Resources**: Connect with local autism support organizations
